⚠️ ETA & EES — sort it early, it takes 10 minutes

Since October 2025, British travellers need an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) to enter the Netherlands. The application costs £10 per person and takes about 10 minutes online at gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice. The key advice from British families who have done it: apply at least two weeks before you travel, not the night before. Some have experienced the app crashing when uploading passport scans. Done early, it is completely stress-free.

💡 Paying in the Netherlands

Most Dutch supermarkets including Albert Heijn do not accept British Visa credit cards. Maestro debit cards and Mastercard work fine. Bring some euros in cash as backup — you will need it at parking meters too.

💬 Language

Almost everyone in Noord-Brabant speaks excellent English. Eindhoven is a major international tech city and the whole region is highly multilingual. The one exception is automated systems like parking meters and some supermarket self-checkouts, which may default to Dutch.

🌡️ When to go

Peak season for families is July and August, with temperatures averaging 22–26°C. June and September are quieter, cheaper and still pleasant. Dutch school holidays partially overlap with UK ones, so parks are lively but not overcrowded.
