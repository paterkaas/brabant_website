Plan your trip to Brabant

Everything a British traveller needs to know before booking, including the post-Brexit essentials.

⚠️ ETA & EES — sort it early, it is simple

Since October 2025, British travellers need an ETA (Electronic Travel Authorisation) to enter the Netherlands. The application takes about 10 minutes online and costs £10 per person. The key tip from British families who have already done it: do it at least two weeks before you travel, not the night before. Some have reported the app crashing when uploading passport scans. Do it early and it is genuinely stress-free.

💡 Paying in the Netherlands

Most Dutch supermarkets including Albert Heijn do not accept British Visa credit cards. Maestro debit cards and Mastercard work fine, or bring some euros in cash as a backup. At the park itself, cards are generally accepted without issue.

🌡️ When to go

The peak season for families is July and August, when temperatures average 22–26°C — comparable to a warm English summer. June and September are quieter, cheaper and still very pleasant. Dutch school holidays partially overlap with UK ones, so the parks are lively but rarely overcrowded.

💬 Language

Almost everyone in Noord-Brabant speaks excellent English. Eindhoven is a major tech city and the area around it is highly international. You will have no difficulty communicating anywhere. The one exception is automated systems — parking meters and some self-checkout tills default to Dutch — so be ready to tap around.
